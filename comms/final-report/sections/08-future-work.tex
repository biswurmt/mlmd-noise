
\section{Future Work}

The most immediate priority is making the Knowledge Graph self-maintaining: a continuous integration pipeline that periodically re-scrapes guideline sources and re-indexes the vector database, paired with a lightweight approval interface where new rule batches require clinician sign-off before entering the live graph. This would close the human-in-the-loop gap without manual reconstruction.

With maintenance in place, the natural next step is transitioning from offline label correction to live prospective decision support. The KG pipeline could infer needed diagnostics from presenting symptoms before physician assessment, directly serving the MLMD's parallelization objective. The GraphRAG pipeline's 175\,ms per-encounter latency is acceptable at SickKids volumes but would require quantization or distilled encoders to scale across multi-facility deployments. At that scale, a 10-point recall improvement across SickKids' 80,000 annual visits redirects roughly 8,000 authorizations per year to the parallelized pathway. Asymmetric label noise from external clinical events is a systematic problem across fragmented hospital networks; the Knowledge Graph and meta-learning framework developed here offers a deployable solution that does not require cross-institutional EHR integration.
