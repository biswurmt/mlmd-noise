\section{Introduction and Clinical Motivation}

At The Hospital for Sick Children (SickKids), children presenting to the emergency department (ED) wait twice: once for a physician to assess them, and again for test results to return. The Machine Learning Medical Directive (MLMD) system~\cite{singh_assessment_2022} eliminates the second wait by predicting which diagnostic tests a patient will need at triage and authorizing them before the physician arrives. A test authorized at triage completes while the patient is still waiting to be seen; a test ordered only after assessment adds another full cycle. The system targets four high-volume pathways: arm X-rays, electrocardiograms (ECGs), appendix ultrasounds, and testicular ultrasounds.

A structural gap in the SickKids electronic health record (EHR) limits what the model can learn. When a patient is transferred to, or later evaluated at, an external facility, any test ordered there never appears in SickKids' records. The hospital's EHR is not integrated with external provider systems. The model observes these missing tests as negatives, and because the missingness tracks transfer patterns rather than clinical need, it learns systematic errors: associating certain presentations with ``test not needed'' when the test was simply performed elsewhere. The production system attempts to correct this with regular-expression matching over clinical notes, but regex cannot capture semantic variants or clinical shorthand, leaving a large class of false negatives in the training data. Across 483,705 triage encounters spanning June 2018 to January 2026, each of these silent absences trains the model toward the same wrong generalization: this presentation does not require testing.

We address this noise at two levels. At the data level, we replace regex matching with a dynamically constructed Knowledge Graph and GraphRAG retrieval pipeline that resolves ambiguous negatives against clinical guidelines before training. At the training level, we apply LiLAW (Lightweight Learnable Adaptive Weighting)~\cite{moturu_lilaw_2025}, a meta-learning framework that down-weights suspected false negatives the imputation step cannot adjudicate. Together, these target asymmetric noise without requiring cross-institutional EHR integration or access to ground-truth transfer records. The Knowledge Graph pipeline recovered 11,110 test-ordering events the regex baseline missed. The sections below present the imputation pipeline evaluation and a controlled validation of LiLAW under noise conditions matching the MLMD structure, establishing the expected performance envelope for the integrated system.
