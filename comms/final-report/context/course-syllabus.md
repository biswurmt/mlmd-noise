## Course Description

Intended for graduate students in Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare (AIH) with technical backgrounds (CS, engineering, statistics, epidemiology). The course establishes an essential foundation of medical concepts and problems, enabling students to take first steps in computational research and AI applications in medicine.

You will acquire basic knowledge about various medical concepts and hands-on experience deploying Machine Learning algorithms. Learn current practices and applications of ML in medicine, and understand what ML can and cannot do for healthcare.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, you will:

- Become familiar with a variety of medical fields and the types of problems addressable with AI
- Develop the ability to critically reason about problems across medical domains, including awareness of knowns and unknowns
- Design and implement effective solutions to specific medical questions
- Communicate clearly with medical experts about both technical nuances and non-technical domain-specific issues
- Present work in a way that clearly articulates its value to intended users
- Structure code so it is shareable, reusable, and aligned with good software-engineering practices
- Situate work within the context of existing research and prior work
- Explain the innovative aspects of a project and justify the choices made during development
- Review and critically evaluate other researchers' work in AI and healthcare

## Course Policies

### Attendance & Participation

Regular attendance and active participation are essential. Students are expected to complete required readings before class and come prepared to contribute thoughtfully to discussion.

### Late Work

Weekly reflection quizzes are due at the specified time. Unless otherwise noted:
- 20% penalty per day for late submissions
- Maximum 5 days late (no credit after 5 days)

### Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be your own and must adhere to University of Toronto's policies on academic integrity. Plagiarism and other forms of academic misconduct will be handled according to university regulations.
